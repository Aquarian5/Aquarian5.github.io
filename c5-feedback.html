Pairing Is Caring

By Courtney Wiggins

August 24, 2015

Learning how to program has been a journey for me. One minute I am on top of the world because my code works, the other I feel overwhelmed. Enrolling as a student in Dev Bootcamp(DBC) has really helped me with my confidence as I learn how to program because of pair programming and feedback. When I heard about this quality of the program, I knew that DBC would be the perfect place for me to flourish as it aligns with my learning style.


From the start, I liked paring because it gave me a chance to learn from someone else and work through problems that I might view as complex. I am very lucky to have paired with partners who are patient, kind, and friendly. This makes for a great session. What I found rewarding is when my partner and I encountered a particularly complex challenge and it took us a while to complete (even over the course of a day or two). When we finally found a solution, we would celebrate our success. These mini celebrations were a boost of confidence for me. Luckily, I have not encountered a paring session that was frustrating. Regarding feedback, when I read my first feedback snippets, I was happy because they were very positive. I felt empowered to continue on. The feedback I received was helpful to my learning. I was able to see some of the flaws I have when it comes to programming. I liked giving feedback because I wanted to write info that would be helpful to my pair and hoped that it would.
