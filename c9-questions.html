Conflict? What Conflict?!

By Courtney Wiggins

September 21, 2015

Unfortunately, if you are human and have feelings, you have experienced a conflict or two in your lifetime. Some conflicts are deeper than others. Some conflicts leave a lasting impression upon you while others are fleeting. Either way, conflicts affect you.

I try not to let things get to me. When I have an issue, I tend to address it in that moment, letting the person(s) that are bothering me know how I feel and why. I like having a conversation about what is bothering me in the moment I feel it because, I know that if I do not, there is a greater chance of me getting angry and/or upset. This can lead to hurt feelings.

Recently, I encountered a conflict with a family member regarding the way they treat another family member. Their treatment of our family member was disrespectful and hurtful to said family members home and spouse. At first, I felt extremely angry and I wanted to lash out at my family member. I did get in a few choice words and my delivery was great lol! However, I did say some stuff that were “below the belt” and immediately stopped myself from saying more hurtful things. That’s where I was wrong. I chose to get angry. I didn’t have to be.

If I could go back in time, I would definitely take a deep breath and remove myself from the situation (go outside, call my sister/friends/etc). Sometimes you can see the bigger picture if you do this (this works in programming too!). I know I hurt feelings that day and it was because I wanted the person to feel the same type of hurt they were afflicting upon my family member, whom I adore. Said family member was unable to defend herself so I thought to do it for her. Next time, I will sit back and assess the situation before I react.
