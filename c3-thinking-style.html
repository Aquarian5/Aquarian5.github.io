I Just Think Differently....And That's OK!

By Courtney Wiggins

August 17, 2015

I must say that the first three weeks of DBC has been an awesome journey. I have been able to connect with other students, learn about HTML5 and CSS3, discover the Ruby basics, and learn how to effectively solve a problem with another person (remotely I should add) through pair programing. I love what I have both learned and acheived so far as I have pushed myself out of my comfort zone completly. It still feels a lot awkward and weird but I am enduring it because my goal is to become a proficient and efficient software developer. I truely believe I will.

When learning, I grasp concepts the best when I hear and/or see them. When this happens, my mind starts to turn...taking what was said and turning the info into pictures, a movie, and or a song. This helps me to remember better. Since learning how to program is an abstract thing, it has been a little difficult for me to make some things 'click.' To solidify my learning, I usually follow up with a video tutorial. I have found youtube to be a wonderful resource for me. Moving forward, I plan on budgeting time to continue to research concepts that I do not understand and continue to not be so hard on myself because I am a beginner and learning programming is hard! I must cut myself some slack. At the end of the day, I am doing the damn thing!

To expand the ways I work and learn, I will try to not rely so heavy on visual and just focus on learning from listening or even just reading. I will set aside time after work and family obligations just for me where I can really hone in on my studies.
